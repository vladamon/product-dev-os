There's a whole cluster of repos doing exactly what you're building. Here are the most worth examining:

---

Most directly inspirational

phuryn/pm-skills — the closest analogue to your project. 100+ agentic skills organized into 8 plugins
covering the full PM lifecycle: discovery → strategy → execution → launch → growth. What's worth studying:

- How they split skills (pedagogic, framework-encoded) vs commands (chained workflows that compose skills)
- Their pm-product-discovery subdirectory models the discover/validate phases you have in your stack
- Each command suggests the next one after it completes — a sequencing pattern your product:next could learn
from

deanpeters/Product-Manager-Skills — fewer skills but denser methodology. Explicitly encodes Teresa Torres
(Continuous Discovery Habits), Geoffrey Moore, Amazon's PRFAQ, Marty Cagan. Good source for the reasoning
behind each step, not just the steps.

---

Lighter but worth a skim

carlvellotti/carls-product-os — solo dev / indie maker orientation similar to yours. Simpler structure,
better signal on what actually matters when you're a team of one.

satyapavan1/ai-pm-os — 14 skills, AI-product-specific focus (PRFAQ, agent design, metrics). Useful if you
eventually want to add AI-specific product skills.

---

What your repo does differently

Your discover → model → journey → shape → spec → build → measure → reflect track is more opinionated about
sequencing than any of the above. Most of them are skill catalogs you pick from; yours enforces a workflow.
That's a meaningful differentiator — lean into it.

The recipes/ + templates/ separation you have is also structurally cleaner than what I see in the others.
